Ruminations and remnants / don't give up, find your circle of influence

don't give up

For more than 40 years, Bernie Sanders has spoken on the wealth disparity issue in the U.S.1 that, unfortunately, not only still exists but has gotten considerably worse2. Seeing this kind of unrelenting persistence, untarnished integrity, and realistic optimism for the future inspires me, especially during times like this week. It's tempting to give up and choose the easier option of succumbing to apathy and enjoying a small but hedonistic life while ignoring the dumpster-fire-world around us. But, Nelson Mandela said that change takes centuries not decades. He also said, "The greatest glory in living lies not in never falling, but in rising every time we fall."

find your circle of influence

Following positive psychology's model of the circles of influence3, focusing on the things we do have control over will help us to build a sense of agency when feeling a general malaise of hopelessness or discouragement. Our brains have limited mental and emotional capacity to care about a finite number of things. We must first focus on what we can control and expand into things we can and want to influence. I plan to use this model to navigate the new administration, and hope you find this helpful too.

  • Control: Our emotions
  • Control: Systems we implement to help regulation our emotions
  • Influence: Our smallest social circle
  • Influence: Our smaller, hyperlocal communities
  • No Control or Influence: Ignore
